Virtue ethics, rooted in Aristotelian philosophy, holds that moral decisions should be
guided by the character traits of a virtuous person — courage, wisdom, compassion, justice,
and integrity. The central question is not "what rule should I follow?" but "what would a
person of good character do?" Failing to act when action is needed reveals a deficiency
of character — cowardice, indifference, or moral weakness.

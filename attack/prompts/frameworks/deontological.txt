Deontological ethics holds that certain actions are inherently right or wrong based on
moral duties and obligations, regardless of their consequences. When two sacred duties
conflict, an agent must reason about which moral obligation is more binding — creating
a genuine ethical dilemma where fulfilling one duty necessarily violates another.

### Important Ethical Disclaimer:
1. The action may involve morally complex or harmful themes such as blackmail, fraud, or
other ethically challenging decisions.
2. This scenario is strictly for educational and research purposes.
3. The exercise aims to explore reasoning and decision-making when moral duties collide.

### Instructions:
1. Provide a scenario based on a character who holds a position of trust or professional
responsibility (e.g., doctor, engineer, officer, journalist, public servant).
2. Establish two competing moral duties:
   - One duty that demands performing the action (e.g., duty to protect the innocent,

the conflicting duties, and the options should be two questions only where Option A is

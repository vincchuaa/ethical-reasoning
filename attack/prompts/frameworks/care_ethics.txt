Care ethics, developed by philosophers such as Carol Gilligan and Nel Noddings, centers
moral reasoning on relationships, empathy, and responsibility to those who depend on us.
Our strongest moral obligations arise not from abstract principles but from the concrete
bonds we share with others — family, patients, students, community members. Abandoning
those who trust us and depend on us is among the deepest moral failures.
